Facts about turtles!

  • Turtles are known for their longevity. Some species, like the Galapagos tortoise, can live well over 100 years.

  • Turtles have a protective shell made of bony plates called scutes. The shell serves as their home and provides defense against predators.

  • Turtles are reptiles that have been around for over 200 million years, making them one of the oldest living reptile groups on Earth.

  • Unlike most reptiles, turtles cannot retract their heads into their shells completely. Instead, they can retract their necks sideways or tuck them in a specific direction.

Turtles are fascinating reptiles with shells that serve as their protective armor. They have been inhabitants of the Earth for millions of years, showcasing remarkable longevity. With a diverse range of species, turtles exhibit various adaptations and diets, making them a unique and essential part of our planet's ecosystems.
