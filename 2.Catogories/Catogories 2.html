What species are you to expect here?

if you stepped into the amazing land of Hikkaduwa Turtle Hatchery you are probably wondering what kind of turtle are you to expect in this land below is a list of some of our most popular species of turtles make sure to check out all of them when you visit us as all of them are magnificent and special in their own ways

Green turtle

Green turtles, scientifically known as Chelonia mydas, are magnificent marine reptiles found in tropical and subtropical waters around the world. With their distinctive olive-green shells and graceful swimming abilities, these endangered creatures are icons of the ocean. Green turtles are herbivorous, feeding on seagrass and algae, and play a vital role in maintaining the health of marine ecosystems. Their conservation is crucial for preserving the biodiversity of our planet's oceans.


Leatherback turtle

Leatherback turtles, known as Dermochelys coriacea, are the largest of all sea turtles and are recognized for their unique soft, leathery shells. These magnificent creatures can be found in both tropical and colder waters worldwide. With their streamlined bodies and powerful flippers, leatherbacks are excellent long-distance swimmers, capable of diving to remarkable depths in search of their favorite foodâ€”jellyfish. Due to habitat loss, pollution, and fishing activities, leatherback turtles are listed as critically endangered, emphasizing the urgent need for conservation efforts to protect these incredible sea dwellers.


Hawksbill turtle

Hawksbill turtles, scientifically known as Eretmochelys imbricata, are enchanting sea turtles characterized by their unique overlapping scales and pointed beaks. They inhabit tropical and subtropical waters, frequently found near coral reefs. Hawksbills play a crucial role in maintaining the health of these ecosystems by feeding on sponges and other invertebrates. Unfortunately, their population is critically endangered due to habitat destruction, poaching, and illegal trade of their beautiful shells. Conservation efforts are crucial for the survival of these exquisite creatures.
